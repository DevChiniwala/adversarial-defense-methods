## Motivation
* Vision-Language Models (VLMs) are increasingly integrated into e-commerce for search, recommendation, and autonomous shopping agents, expanding the attack surface.
* Subtle adversarial perturbations applied to product images can mislead recommendation engines, causing severe financial and reputational damage.
* Open-source and commercial VLMs display significant differential robustness; the visual modality is consistently found to be the weakest link.
* Traditional OCR and text-based content filters are insufficient against typographic and multimodal structural exploits native to e-commerce catalogs.

## Contribution
* Demonstrates high-success-rate gradient-based attacks (BIM, PGD) specifically tailored to compromise VLM agents in simulated e-commerce environments.
* Highlights severe modality-specific weaknesses by showing how visual perturbations propagate and corrupt cross-modal semantic reasoning.
* Proposes an end-to-end anomaly detection and modality-specific adversarial training pipeline tailored for commercial catalog data.
* Sets a new state-of-the-art benchmark for red-teaming shopping agents prior to commercial deployment.
