## Motivation
* As quantum computing advances, classical cryptographic and machine learning systems face existential threats, including quantum-enhanced adversarial attacks that can optimize perturbations exponentially faster.\n* Current adversarial ML literature operates almost exclusively under the assumption of classical computational bounds for the adversary.\n* There is a lack of comprehensive threat models and defensive strategies designed to secure ML infrastructure against adversaries equipped with fault-tolerant quantum computers.

## Contribution
* Formalizes the threat model for Post-Quantum Adversarial ML, detailing how quantum algorithms (e.g., Grover's algorithm variants) can accelerate adversarial example generation.\n* Proposes a novel defense strategy utilizing quantum-resistant noise injection and lattice-based cryptographic priors to obfuscate the model's gradient landscape.\n* Provides theoretical proofs demonstrating that the proposed defense increases the lower bound of queries required for a successful quantum-enhanced attack to exponential time.
