## Motivation
* Traditional adversarial robustness research heavily focused on single-modality models (like CNNs) where small pixel perturbations led to misclassification.
* With the advent of Large Vision-Language Models (VLMs), the attack surface has fundamentally shifted and expanded, demanding new robustness metrics.
* Simple model scaling has proven insufficient to inherently guarantee robustness against multimodal exploits, highlighting a persistent research gap.
* Standard benchmark defenses often fail to generalize to cross-modal interactions, leaving critical vulnerabilities in real-world deployment.

## Contribution
* Provides a comprehensive systematization of adversarial vulnerabilities across the transition from CNNs to modern VLMs.
* Introduces a unified taxonomy for multimodal attacks, including adversarial prompt tuning and geometric perturbation detection.
* Proposes a novel theoretical framework to measure geometric inconsistencies across embedding spaces to detect stealthy attacks.
* Demonstrates state-of-the-art evaluation methodologies highlighting how cross-modal vulnerabilities bypass existing single-modality safety checkers.
