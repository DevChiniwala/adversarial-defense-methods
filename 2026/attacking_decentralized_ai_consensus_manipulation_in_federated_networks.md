## Motivation
* Decentralized Federated Learning (DFL) removes central servers to enhance privacy, but shifts critical security vulnerabilities directly to the peer-to-peer consensus layer.
* Byzantine adversaries can exploit distributed consensus protocols to inject poisoned models or backdoors, silently degrading global network performance.
* Traditional outlier detection algorithms fail against sophisticated attacks that manipulate economic incentives or voting mechanisms in blockchain-based DFL systems.
* The lack of central oversight creates opportunities for malicious nodes to execute distributed inference attacks, reconstructing sensitive local data from model updates.

## Contribution
* Formulates a comprehensive threat model for direct consensus manipulation and model poisoning in heterogeneous peer-to-peer federated networks.
* Demonstrates how Byzantine nodes can dominate gossip-based and blockchain-based aggregation protocols without triggering standard anomaly detectors.
* Introduces a novel trust-aware consensus mechanism (FedTrust) that dynamically weights peer updates based on cryptographic proofs and historical reputation.
* Evaluates advanced defense strategies including Secure Multi-Party Computation (SMPC) to guarantee authentic, robust, and private decentralized aggregation.
