## Motivation
* Agentic LLMs operate with greater autonomy than traditional chatbots, introducing severe security challenges like tool exploitation and memory poisoning.
* Existing component-level defenses (e.g., standard input filtering) fail to protect against complex, multi-step attacks in autonomous workflows.
* There is a lack of structured frameworks that map adaptive threats to specific design dimensions (e.g., long-term memory, inter-agent communication).
* Time-of-check-to-time-of-use (TOCTOU) flaws and inter-agent trust vulnerabilities remain largely unaddressed in current architectures.

## Contribution
* Formulates a comprehensive systematization of agentic defenses spanning three pillars: Design Dimensions, Threat Taxonomies, and Defense Frameworks.
* Introduces the concept of "agentic firewalls" and formal policy verification to maintain integrity across multi-agent interactions.
* Proposes a novel standardized benchmark for evaluating defenses against adaptive, real-world prompt injection and memory corruption scenarios.
* Outlines cryptographically secure protocols for tool calling to prevent unauthorized escalation of privileges.
