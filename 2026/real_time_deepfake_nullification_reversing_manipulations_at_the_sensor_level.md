## Motivation
* The rapid proliferation of highly realistic deepfakes poses severe threats to digital identity, media integrity, and biometric authentication.\n* Current deepfake detection systems are fundamentally reactive, operating post-capture and often engaging in a losing arms race against increasingly sophisticated generative models.\n* There is a lack of proactive, real-time mechanisms that can physically or computationally neutralize manipulations at the point of data capture before the image is fully processed.

## Contribution
* Proposes a novel hardware-software co-design that integrates adversarial noise patterns directly into the camera sensor's image signal processor (ISP).\n* Develops a 'nullification algorithm' that actively disrupts the latent space encoding required by common facial manipulation models, rendering the captured data unusable for deepfake generation.\n* Evaluates the system on modern smartphone camera architectures, showing that it completely prevents real-time face-swapping attacks while preserving the visual quality of the unmanipulated image.
