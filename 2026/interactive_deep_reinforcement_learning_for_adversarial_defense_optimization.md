## Motivation
* Current adversarial defense mechanisms often rely on static, pre-defined threat models that fail to generalize against dynamic and adaptive attackers.\n* Deep Reinforcement Learning (DRL) has shown promise in learning robust policies, but traditional DRL setups struggle with the non-stationary nature of minimax adversarial games.\n* There is a lack of human-in-the-loop approaches that allow domain experts to interactively guide the RL agent's exploration space to avoid catastrophic forgetting and overfitting to specific attack vectors.

## Contribution
* Proposes an Interactive Adversarial DRL (IADRL) framework that integrates human feedback constraints into the reward function to guide defense optimization.\n* Introduces a novel adaptive regularization term that dynamically balances exploration of new attack surfaces and exploitation of known defensive strategies.\n* Achieves state-of-the-art robust accuracy on CIFAR-10 and ImageNet against AutoAttack, while reducing the required training epochs by 40% compared to standard adversarial training.
