## Motivation
* 4D scene understanding, which incorporates 3D spatial data over time (e.g., LiDAR point cloud sequences), is crucial for autonomous driving and robotics.\n* Current 4D perception models are highly vulnerable to adversarial perturbations, such as imperceptible temporal spoofing or small spatial shifts in point clouds, which can lead to catastrophic misclassifications.\n* Existing point cloud defense mechanisms focus primarily on static 3D frames and fail to account for the temporal consistency exploited by 4D adversarial attacks.

## Contribution
* Proposes a spatio-temporal consistency regularization module that enforces kinematic constraints across sequential point cloud frames, detecting anomalies caused by adversarial attacks.\n* Introduces a robust 4D contrastive pre-training objective that improves the inherent adversarial resilience of the learned spatio-temporal representations.\n* Demonstrates a 35% improvement in robust accuracy on the Waymo Open Dataset against state-of-the-art 4D adversarial attacks, setting a new benchmark for secure perception in autonomous systems.
