## Motivation
* Spatio-Temporal Graph Neural Networks (STGNNs) are widely used in critical infrastructure such as traffic forecasting and power grid management, making them high-value targets for adversaries.\n* While certified robustness has been extensively studied for image classifiers and static GNNs, verifying STGNNs is inherently difficult due to the complex interplay of temporal dynamics and spatial graph convolutions.\n* Empirical defenses against structural and temporal perturbations in STGNNs do not provide theoretical guarantees, leaving systems vulnerable to worst-case adaptive attacks.

## Contribution
* Provides the first theoretical framework for certified robustness in STGNNs based on an adapted randomized smoothing technique that accounts for both spatial topology and temporal sequences.\n* Derives tight deterministic bounds for Lipschitz-continuous temporal aggregation modules, enabling scalable verification of large-scale dynamic graphs.\n* Evaluates the framework on real-world traffic and energy datasets, providing guaranteed safety margins against adversarial node injections and feature perturbations.
