# 8. Universal and Transferable Adversarial Attacks on Aligned Language Models (Zou et al., 2023)

## 🤔 Why You Must Read This Paper
This is the paper that brought adversarial attacks to the ChatGPT era. Before this, most LLM 'jailbreaks' were manual, clever prompts written by humans. This paper mathematically optimized an attack string that could reliably break the safety guardrails of modern LLMs.

## 💡 What It Contributed to the Field
It introduced the Greedy Coordinate Gradient (GCG) algorithm. By appending a seemingly random string of gibberish characters (calculated via gradients) to a malicious prompt, it bypassed the alignment of models like Llama-2 and ChatGPT. It proved that LLMs suffer from the exact same fundamental vulnerabilities as image classifiers.
