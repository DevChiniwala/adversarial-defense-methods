# 9. Nightshade: Prompt-Specific Poisoning Attacks on Text-to-Image Generative Models (Shan et al., 2024)

## 🤔 Why You Must Read This Paper
As Generative AI (like Midjourney and DALL-E) exploded, artists were furious that their work was being scraped for training data without consent. This paper is a must-read because it flipped adversarial ML into an offensive weapon *for* the public to protect their copyright.

## 💡 What It Contributed to the Field
It introduced a data poisoning attack that allows artists to invisibly alter their images before uploading them. If an AI company scrapes these 'poisoned' images, it irreparably breaks the AI's ability to generate specific concepts (e.g., asking for a 'dog' generates a 'cat'). It represents a huge shift in the ethical application of adversarial attacks.
