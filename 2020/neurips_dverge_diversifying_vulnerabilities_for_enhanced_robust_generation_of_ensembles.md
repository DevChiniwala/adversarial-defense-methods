## Motivation
* While adversarial attacks have grown increasingly sophisticated, standard defensive strategies like adversarial training suffer from severe computational overhead.
* Existing defenses often face a steep trade-off between adversarial robustness and clean dataset accuracy.
* The community requires more robust, generalizable defense mechanisms that can withstand adaptive and unseen threats.

## Contribution
* Introduces a robust defensive architecture that significantly improves model resilience against a wide array of adversarial examples.
* Provides theoretical and empirical evidence showing that the proposed method minimizes the degradation of clean accuracy.
* Establishes a new benchmark for robust optimization in machine learning pipelines.

## Summary
This research presents a novel defense mechanism aimed at hardening neural networks against adversarial attacks. The proposed framework successfully mitigates the impact of adversarial perturbations while maintaining high performance on clean, unperturbed data.
