## Motivation
* Deep neural networks remain highly susceptible to adversarial perturbations, yet existing attacks often lack efficiency or fail against modern defenses.
* Prior attack methodologies frequently assume white-box access, making them less applicable to real-world, black-box scenarios.
* There is a critical need to evaluate model robustness against more sophisticated, transferrable adversarial examples.

## Contribution
* Proposes a novel, highly efficient adversarial attack framework that generates imperceptible perturbations.
* Demonstrates state-of-the-art attack success rates and high transferability across different network architectures.
* Extensively evaluates the approach against current defensive mechanisms, proving its superiority in bypassing them.

## Summary
This paper introduces a powerful new adversarial attack strategy designed to expose critical vulnerabilities in modern deep learning systems. By optimizing the perturbation generation process, the authors achieve high transferability and success rates, challenging existing defensive paradigms.
